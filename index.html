Who This Guide Is For

Built for adults who’ve been through something.

This is not a guide for people starting from zero. It’s for people who were active — or wanted to be — and have hit a wall because of pain, age, or injury.

  • You’ve recently completed physical therapy and aren’t sure what to do next
  • You have a chronic condition — back pain, joint stiffness, an old surgery — that makes standard workout programs feel off-limits
  • You’re in your 30s, 40s, 50s, or 60s and noticing your body recovering differently than it used to
  • You’re a busy professional or parent with limited time, and you need a plan that’s realistic — not aspirational
  • You’ve tried returning to exercise before and stopped because something started hurting
  • You want to feel more capable, stable, and strong in daily life — not just look a certain way

This guide may not be right for you if: You’re currently in acute pain or have an undiagnosed injury. Please work with your physician or physical therapist before beginning any exercise program. This guide is for people who have received clearance to exercise and are looking for a structured, careful way to rebuild.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is this guide safe if I’m currently managing chronic pain?

This guide is designed for people who have medical clearance to exercise and are looking for a careful, structured return to movement. It is not a substitute for medical advice and is not intended for people in acute pain or with undiagnosed conditions. If you’re unsure, speak with your doctor or physical therapist first.

Do I need any equipment?

The exercises are designed to be performed with minimal equipment. A resistance band and a sturdy chair are helpful for some movements, but most can be done using your own bodyweight. The guide includes notes on what’s needed for each exercise.

I finished physical therapy months ago. Is this still relevant for me?

Yes — this guide is specifically useful for people in the gap between completing formal rehabilitation and resuming normal exercise. Physical therapy addresses the injury; this guide is about rebuilding general strength and function from that baseline. It’s a common and often unaddressed phase.

What if I have multiple limitations — for example, both knee and back issues?

The guide includes modification guidance for the most common limitations, including knee, hip, and lower back sensitivities. For people with multiple concurrent limitations, it may be most effective as a supplement to working with a qualified trainer or therapist who can tailor programming to your specific situation.

Is this actually free? What’s the catch?

Yes — it’s free. No hidden charge, no trial period, no automatic enrollment. You’ll receive the guide and may occasionally hear from TC Fitness with relevant content. You can unsubscribe at any time. We’d rather earn your trust with something useful than pressure you into something you’re not ready for.

How is TC Fitness different from a standard personal trainer or online program?

TC Fitness specifically works with adults who have pain histories, physical limitations, or post-rehabilitation needs. This is a different skill set from general fitness coaching. Our approach is slower, more careful, and more individualized than most standard programs — and it’s designed to produce results that last.
